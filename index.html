Business & Strategy

Leads asCEO

Your Corporate House is a living company. You'll split into three departments, bid for a real-world brand at auction, and run it under the pressure of live tasks — orchestrated by your CEO, not micromanaged by them.

Three Departments

1Advertising & Marketing

The bridge between company and public — builds campaigns, posters, and messages that make the product recognisable, exciting, and trusted.

2Corporate Social Responsibility

Design a self-sustaining social enterprise around a real issue — education, environment, health, equality — that earns enough to keep making impact.

3Intrapreneurship

Pitch a new for-profit idea from inside the Corporate House: the problem it solves, who it helps, how it earns, and why it's innovative.

Media & Communication

Leads asCEMO

Your Media House is the fourth pillar of this simulation — watching, verifying, and reporting on everything the Corporate, Political, and Law Houses do. Fast-paced, occasionally chaotic, always essential.

Three Departments

1News Department

The heart of the house — gathers information, verifies facts, runs interviews, and reports clearly and without bias.

2Editorial & Ethics

Reviews every story for fairness, balance, and sensitivity — deciding how a story is framed, and whether it runs at all.

3Digital & Media Innovation

Owns how the news actually reaches people — social strategy, headlines, short-form video, and new digital formats.

The bigger picture

Compete within your world. Win by working across it.

Neither house battles the other directly — Business competes with Business, Media with Media. But the top spot only comes to teams that lean on the divisions around them.

LEADERSHIP

Responsibility, not advantage

A CEO or CEMO isn't the person with the most power — they're the one who ensures everything gets done. Orchestrate, delegate, and empower; step in only when it truly matters.

MENTORSHIP

Mentors first, judges later

Every department mentor is there to guide you through live tasks and deadlines — ask them anything. The goal is to learn while you perform, never to feel lost.

COLLABORATION

Cross-house interaction

Media covers Business announcements; Business responds to press coverage. The simulation only feels real when both houses treat each other as part of the same world.
